The Olympic Flame

The Olympic flame for the 2024 Paris Games was lit on April 16, 2024, during a ceremonial event held at the ancient site of Olympia, Greece, a tradition that dates back to the inception of the modern Olympics. The flame symbolizes peace, unity, and the enduring spirit of the Games. It was ignited using the sun's rays, concentrated with a parabolic mirror by an actress portraying the high priestess. This ritual, rooted in ancient Greek traditions, reflects the connection between the ancient and modern Games.

Following the lighting, the flame embarked on a journey, starting with an 11-day relay through Greece before being handed over to the Paris 2024 organizing committee in Athens. It will arrive in France on May 8, after which it will be carried across the country, culminating in its arrival at the Olympic Stadium in Paris for the opening ceremony on July 26.
