The Olympic Flame

The Olympic flame for the 2024 Paris Games was lit on April 16, 2024, during a ceremonial event held at the ancient site of Olympia, Greece, a tradition that dates back to the inception of the modern Olympics. The flame symbolizes peace, unity, and the enduring spirit of the Games. It was ignited using the sun's rays, concentrated with a parabolic mirror by an actress portraying the high priestess.

Following the lighting, the flame embarked on a journey, starting with an 11-day relay through Greece before being handed over to the Paris 2024 organizing committee in Athens. It will arrived in France on May 8, after which it was carried across the country, culminating in its arrival at the Olympic Stadium in Paris for the opening ceremony on July 26. The flame’s journey through France is part of a broader effort to celebrate the Games' values of inclusivity, sustainability, and peace.

The Opening Ceremony

A Celebration of Unity, Diversity and Inclusivity

Flags- Transcending the Gender Gap


The 2024 Olympics were the first edition of the olympics to require countries to have flag-bearer of both genders- a massive step towards gender equality and equal representation of men and women in sports culture.

Music- An Expression of Diversity

The 2024 Paris Olympics opening ceremony showcased musical diversity with Lady Gaga performing “Mon truc en plumes,” Celine Dion singing atop the Eiffel Tower, and French-Malian star Aya Nakamura. The event spanned genres, including rapper Snoop Dogg, who added a unique flair with a surprise performance, alongside French metal band Gojira at the Bastille. The lineup reflected unity through music, highlighting global and cultural collaboration

Closing Ceremony: A Celebration of Unity and Triumph



As the curtain falls on the Paris 2024 Olympic Games, the Closing Ceremony will be a breathtaking celebration of global unity, athletic triumph, and the Olympic spirit. Set against the backdrop of Paris’ iconic Stade de France, the world will come together once more to bid farewell to the Games and honor the athletes who have inspired us all.

The Highlights of the Ceremony

Expect a night of spectacular performances, emotional tributes, and unforgettable moments that will resonate across the globe.


  • Artistic Performances: The Closing Ceremony will showcase France's rich cultural heritage with performances by world-renowned artists, musicians, and dancers. Experience a fusion of classical and modern French art that celebrates Paris as a cultural hub.
  • The Parade of Nations: Athletes from over 200 countries will walk hand in hand, united by the values of friendship, excellence, and respect. This parade symbolizes the end of the competition and the continuation of the Olympic journey beyond borders.
  • The Transfer of the Olympic Flag: As tradition dictates, the Olympic flag will be handed over to the next host city for the 2028 Games, Los Angeles. Witness the symbolic moment when the Olympic legacy is passed from one iconic city to another, setting the stage for the next chapter in Olympic history.
